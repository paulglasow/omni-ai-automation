# Non-Technical Deployment Guide

This guide provides step-by-step instructions for all phases of deployment in a clear and straightforward manner, suitable for non-technical stakeholders.

## Phase 1: Planning
1. **Identify Objectives**: Determine what you hope to achieve with this deployment.
2. **Gather Requirements**: Collect all necessary information and resources needed for the deployment.
3. **Create a Timeline**: Develop a schedule for when each part of the deployment will take place.
4. **Assign Roles**: Clearly define who is responsible for each task during the deployment.

## Phase 2: Preparation
1. **Set Up Infrastructure**: Ensure that all required systems and tools are in place.
2. **Train Staff**: Provide training sessions to ensure that everyone knows what to expect during the deployment.
3. **Test Everything**: Perform dry runs of the deployment process to identify any potential issues.

## Phase 3: Deployment
1. **Inform Stakeholders**: Notify all relevant parties that the deployment will begin.
2. **Execute the Deployment**: Follow the established plan to carry out the deployment.
3. **Monitor Progress**: Keep an eye on the deployment to catch any issues as they arise.

## Phase 4: Post-Deployment
1. **Verify Functionality**: Check that everything is working as expected after the deployment.
2. **Gather Feedback**: Collect input from users and stakeholders about the deployment experience.
3. **Address Any Issues**: Resolve any problems that were identified during the verification process.

## Phase 5: Review and Documentation
1. **Document the Process**: Record what went well and what could be improved for future deployments.
2. **Create a Lessons Learned Report**: Summarize key takeaways from the deployment for future reference.
3. **Update Relevant Documentation**: Ensure that all related documents reflect the changes made during the deployment.
